Custom Postcards by Tyler

Welcome! If you've landed on this page, you likely received a postcard from me through Postcrossing. You received a custom-made postcard from me -- either a photo I took or a linocut I made. I hope you enjoy learning more about the place (or object) on your postcard. Have fun on your Postcrossing journey!

Postcard 01

Kodiak brown bear in river

I took this photo on Kodiak Island in Alaska, where I spent the summer of 2012 working with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. This brown bear was fishing along a stream at this approximate location: 57.2575, -154.2798.

Postcard 02

Karluk Lake, Alaska

I took this photo on Kodiak Island in Alaska, where I spent the summer of 2012 working with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. These boats are on Karluk Lake, and I took the photo from a small island named Camp Island (approximate location: 57.367144, -154.028622).

Postcard 03

Bald eagle on Kodiak Island

I took this photo on Kodiak Island in Alaska, where I spent the summer of 2012 working with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. This bald eagle was flying off from the banks of a stream. Eagles would often perch in the trees beside streams after cleaning up the salmon carcasses that bears had left behind after fishing (approximate location: 57.255915, -154.115458).

Postcard 04

Sockeye fins

I took this photo on Kodiak Island in Alaska, where I spent the summer of 2012 working with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. These are sockeye salmon swimming up a stream to spawn. They spawn and die in the same location where they hatched. At the peak of the salmon runs, the streams looked like red carpets because there were so many salmon. Approximate location: 57.255915, -154.115458.

Postcard 11

Greenland flora

I took this photo near Kangerlussuaq, Greenland, where I spent the summer of 2013 doing climate change research. Our research site (approximate location: 67.095629, -50.277724) was a few kilometers from the edge of the Greenland ice sheet. We were researching the effects of a warming climate on plant growth.

Postcard 12

Greenland glaciers

I took this photo near Kangerlussuaq, Greenland, where I spent the summer of 2013 doing climate change research. This is at the edge of the Greenland ice sheet (approximate location: 67.088255, -50.231839). At night, I would often hear thunder-link sounds from huge chunks of ice falling from the glacier into the water.

Postcard 13

Stomp stomp

This is a linocut I made of a spotted lanternfly, an invasive species that arrived in Philadelphia several years ago. There's a public campaign to encourage people to stomp on the bugs when they see them. Native to Asia, the spotted lanternfly feeds on crops and can harm native plants.

Postcard 14

Duke's mayo

This is a linocut I made of Duke's Mayonnaise, a mayo brand that's popular in the southern United States. I don't live in the South anymore, but it's still my favorite brand of mayo and it makes me think of home.

Comfort & Floyd
